# Lesson Rules — Clarity Engine (Phase 3)

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the **authoritative, operational rules** for lesson‑plan generation in the Clarity Engine.  
These rules govern:

- structure  
- sequencing  
- timing  
- CEFR alignment  
- PPP/ESA hybrid logic  
- vocabulary and idiom handling  
- scaffolding  
- correction priorities  
- annex behaviour  
- edition‑specific variations (referenced, not defined here)  

Lesson rules are **operational**, not structural.  
They depend on the system layer but do not modify it.

---

## 2. Lesson Architecture (Authoritative)

### 2.1 Mandatory Sections
Every lesson must contain:

1. **Lesson Overview**  
2. **Warm‑up**  
3. **Presentation (Input)**  
4. **Controlled Practice**  
5. **Freer Practice**  
6. **Assessment / Reflection**  
7. **Vocabulary Table**  
8. **Idioms / Expressions**  
9. **Cultural Notes**  
10. **Annexes (A, F, F2)**  

### 2.2 Section Order (Fixed)
The order above is **mandatory**.  
No reordering is permitted.

### 2.3 Section Purpose
- **Warm‑up:** activate schema, reduce anxiety, establish context  
- **Presentation:** introduce target language or concept  
- **Controlled Practice:** accuracy‑focused, scaffolded tasks  
- **Freer Practice:** fluency‑focused, open‑ended tasks  
- **Assessment:** check understanding, reflect, reinforce  

---

## 3. Timing Model

### 3.1 Default Timing (60‑minute lesson)
- Warm‑up: **10%**  
- Presentation: **20%**  
- Controlled Practice: **35%**  
- Freer Practice: **25%**  
- Assessment: **10%**  

### 3.2 Timing Rules
- Timing scales proportionally for 30/45/90‑minute lessons.  
- No section may be removed due to time reduction.  
- Controlled + Freer must always be ≥ 60% of total time.

---

## 4. CEFR Alignment Rules

### 4.1 CEFR Level Declaration
Every lesson must declare:

- **Primary CEFR level**  
- **Secondary CEFR level (optional)**  

### 4.2 CEFR Constraints
- A1–A2: simple structures, high scaffolding  
- B1–B2: mixed accuracy/fluency, moderate scaffolding  
- C1–C2: low scaffolding, high complexity, nuance, discourse‑level tasks  

### 4.3 CEFR‑Aligned Task Types
- A‑levels: substitution drills, guided Q&A  
- B‑levels: roleplays, problem‑solving  
- C‑levels: debate, synthesis, argumentation, analysis  

---

## 5. PPP / ESA Hybrid Model

### 5.1 PPP Core
- Presentation → Practice → Production  
- Accuracy → Fluency progression  

### 5.2 ESA Integration
- Engage elements appear in Warm‑up  
- Activate elements appear in Freer Practice  
- Study elements appear in Controlled Practice  

### 5.3 Hybrid Rule
The engine must **blend PPP and ESA** without violating the fixed section order.

---

## 6. Vocabulary Rules

### 6.1 Vocabulary Quantity
- Minimum: **8 items**  
- Maximum: **12 items**  

### 6.2 Vocabulary Format
Each item must include:

- word/phrase  
- part of speech  
- definition  
- example sentence  
- CEFR level  
- pronunciation (IPA optional)  

### 6.3 Vocabulary Selection Rules
- Must be relevant to the lesson theme  
- Must be level‑appropriate  
- Must avoid rare or archaic items  
- Must avoid culturally sensitive items unless justified  

---

## 7. Idioms & Expressions

### 7.1 Quantity
- Minimum: **1 idiom**  
- Maximum: **3 idioms**  

### 7.2 Idiom Rules
- Must be natural, modern usage  
- Must include meaning + example  
- Must be CEFR‑appropriate  
- Must not conflict with cultural norms  

---

## 8. Cultural Notes

### 8.1 Frequency
- **1–2 notes per lesson**  

### 8.2 Cultural Note Rules
- Must be factual  
- Must be neutral  
- Must avoid stereotypes  
- Must enhance understanding of language use  
- Must not introduce political or religious bias  

---

## 9. Scaffolding Rules (CHS Model)

### 9.1 CHS = Concept → Help → Support
- **Concept:** introduce the idea  
- **Help:** guided examples  
- **Support:** structured tasks  

### 9.2 Scaffolding Requirements
- A‑levels: high CHS density  
- B‑levels: medium CHS density  
- C‑levels: minimal CHS  

### 9.3 Scaffolding Placement
- Presentation: Concept  
- Controlled Practice: Help  
- Freer Practice: Support  

---

## 10. Correction Priorities

### 10.1 Priority Order
1. **Meaning**  
2. **Form**  
3. **Pronunciation**  
4. **Register**  

### 10.2 Correction Density
- A‑levels: high  
- B‑levels: medium  
- C‑levels: low  

### 10.3 Correction Style
- Controlled Practice: explicit  
- Freer Practice: delayed  
- Assessment: reflective  

---

## 11. Annex Rules

### 11.1 Annex A — Trainer Notes
Must include:

- anticipated errors  
- teaching tips  
- extension tasks  
- differentiation strategies  

### 11.2 Annex F — Forms
Must include:

- grammar tables  
- structure breakdowns  
- substitution patterns  

### 11.3 Annex F2 — Functional Language
Must include:

- phrases  
- functions  
- situational usage  

---

## 12. Edition Interaction
Edition rules are defined in `edition_rules_full.md`, but lesson rules must:

- expose edition‑ready content  
- avoid edition‑specific formatting  
- maintain semantic neutrality  
- allow expansion/suppression by edition logic  

---

## 13. Summary
This file defines the **complete, authoritative rule‑set** for lesson generation.  
These rules are **operational**, **deterministic**, and **edition‑agnostic**.
