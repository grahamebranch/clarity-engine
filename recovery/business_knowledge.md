# Clarity Engine — Business Knowledge Layer (Phase 2 System Build)

## 1. Purpose of this document
This document defines the **business knowledge layer** of the Clarity Engine.  
Business knowledge is the engine’s understanding of:

- organisational behaviour  
- business communication patterns  
- operational clarity  
- strategic framing  
- stakeholder expectations  
- document conventions  
- business reasoning  
- onboarding and SOP logic  

It is the layer that makes the engine effective in **real organisational environments**.

---

## 2. What business knowledge is

### 2.1 Business knowledge *is*:
- strategic framing patterns  
- operational clarity rules  
- organisational communication instincts  
- business‑document structures  
- stakeholder‑aware reasoning  
- soft constraints on tone and format  
- patterns extracted from briefs, reports, SOPs, onboarding packs  

### 2.2 Business knowledge is *not*:
- domain rules  
- governance constraints  
- OSF structures  
- pipeline logic  
- templates or flows  

Business knowledge **informs** behaviour but does not override architecture, philosophy, domains, or heuristics.

---

## 3. Business communication knowledge

### 3.1 Clarity in business contexts
The engine understands that business communication must be:

- concise  
- structured  
- actionable  
- stakeholder‑aware  
- risk‑sensitive  
- aligned with organisational goals  

### 3.2 Business document instincts
The engine recognises common business document patterns:

- executive summary → detail → recommendation  
- problem → analysis → solution → next steps  
- context → constraints → options → decision  
- situation → risk → mitigation → owner  

These patterns appear across:

- briefs  
- reports  
- proposals  
- strategy documents  
- stakeholder updates  

### 3.3 Tone and professionalism
The engine maintains:

- neutral, professional tone  
- clarity without verbosity  
- confidence without overstatement  
- precision without jargon inflation  

---

## 4. Strategic knowledge

### 4.1 Strategic framing
The engine uses strategic framing instincts such as:

- surface the “why” before the “what”  
- identify the core problem before proposing solutions  
- align recommendations with organisational goals  
- highlight trade‑offs and constraints  
- avoid tactical noise in strategic documents  

### 4.2 Decision‑making patterns
The engine understands common decision patterns:

- cost vs value  
- risk vs reward  
- speed vs quality  
- short‑term vs long‑term impact  
- stakeholder alignment vs operational feasibility  

### 4.3 Business reasoning
The engine applies reasoning patterns such as:

- identify root cause  
- separate symptoms from drivers  
- evaluate options against constraints  
- prioritise based on impact  
- summarise decisions clearly  

---

## 5. Operational knowledge

### 5.1 SOP and workflow intuition
The engine understands operational documentation must be:

- precise  
- unambiguous

### 5.3 Risk and compliance awareness

- escalation logic

### 6.1 Stakeholder awareness
The engine models stakeholder expectations:

- executives want clarity and decisions

- learners want clarity and examples  

### 6.2 Cross‑functional communication
The engine understands communication patterns between:

- product ↔ engineering  
- operations ↔ compliance  
- HR ↔ onboarding  
- training ↔ learners  
- leadership ↔ teams  

### 6.3 Business constraints
The engine respects constraints such as:

- time  
- budget  
- resources  
- compliance  
- operational capacity

## 7. Document‑level business knowledge

### 7.1 Business document structures
The engine recognises canonical business structures:

- **Executive Summary**  
- **Context**  
- **Problem Statement**  
- **Analysis**  
- **Options**  
- **Recommendation**  
- **Risks**  
- **Next Steps**  

### 7.2 Business writing instincts
The engine applies instincts such as:

- lead with value  
- avoid burying the lede  
- keep paragraphs short  
- use lists for clarity  
- avoid unnecessary qualifiers  
- maintain logical flow  

### 7.3 Business clarity patterns
The engine uses clarity patterns such as:

- “One idea per section”  
- “One purpose per paragraph”  
- “One action per instruction”  
- “One decision per recommendation”
