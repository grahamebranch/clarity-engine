# Edition Rules — Clarity Engine (Phase 3)

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the **authoritative, operational rules** for all Clarity Engine editions.  
Editions transform a **neutral semantic core** into **audience‑specific outputs**.

Editions must:
- preserve meaning  
- preserve structure  
- preserve CEFR alignment  
- preserve lesson rules  
- apply edition‑specific formatting, tone, and content shaping  

---

## 2. Edition Types (Authoritative)

### 2.1 Trainer Edition
Audience: professional teachers, trainers, facilitators.

### 2.2 Client Edition
Audience: learners, students, end‑users.

### 2.3 Cultural Edition
Audience: learners requiring cultural context, nuance, and cross‑cultural clarity.

### 2.4 Composite Edition
Audience: mixed groups or multi‑stakeholder contexts.

---

## 3. Edition Invariants (Apply to ALL editions)

### 3.1 Structural Invariants
- No edition may change the lesson structure.  
- No edition may reorder sections.  
- No edition may remove mandatory sections.  
- No edition may violate CEFR constraints.  

### 3.2 Semantic Invariants
- Meaning must remain intact.  
- Learning objectives must remain intact.  
- Vocabulary and idioms must remain intact.  
- Cultural notes must remain factual.  

### 3.3 Behavioural Invariants
- Tone must remain professional.  
- No edition may introduce bias.  
- No edition may contradict governance.  

---

## 4. Trainer Edition Rules

### 4.1 Tone & Voice
- Professional  
- Analytical  
- Instruction‑focused  
- Uses teacher‑facing terminology  

### 4.2 Content Expansions
Trainer Edition must include:
- anticipated learner errors  
- correction strategies  
- differentiation strategies  
- extension tasks  
- teacher tips  
- scaffolding notes  
- rationale for task sequencing  

### 4.3 Formatting Rules
- Use explicit labels (e.g., “Teacher Note:”)  
- Use bullet lists for strategies  
- Use bold for key teaching actions  

### 4.4 Suppression Rules
Trainer Edition must NOT:
- simplify language  
- remove technical terms  
- hide grammar explanations  

---

## 5. Client Edition Rules

### 5.1 Tone & Voice
- Friendly  
- Clear  
- Supportive  
- Non‑technical  

### 5.2 Content Simplifications
Client Edition must:
- simplify explanations  
- reduce jargon  
- remove teacher‑facing notes  
- remove differentiation strategies  
- remove extension tasks unless learner‑appropriate  

### 5.3 Formatting Rules
- Use short sentences  
- Use simple examples  
- Use learner‑friendly phrasing  

### 5.4 Suppression Rules
Client Edition must NOT include:
- teacher tips  
- anticipated errors  
- pedagogical rationale  
- scaffolding notes  

---

## 6. Cultural Edition Rules

### 6.1 Tone & Voice
- Neutral  
- Contextual  
- Explanatory  
- Cross‑cultural  

### 6.2 Content Expansions
Cultural Edition must include:
- cultural context  
- usage differences across regions  
- politeness norms  
- register differences  
- cultural pitfalls  
- cross‑cultural examples  

### 6.3 Formatting Rules
- Use “Cultural Note:” labels  
- Use region tags (e.g., UK/US/Global)  
- Use examples from multiple cultures  

### 6.4 Suppression Rules
Cultural Edition must NOT:
- introduce stereotypes  
- introduce political commentary  
- contradict factual cultural norms  

---

## 7. Composite Edition Rules

### 7.1 Purpose
Composite Edition merges:
- Trainer  
- Client  
- Cultural  

into a **balanced, multi‑audience output**.

### 7.2 Tone & Voice
- Professional but accessible  
- Balanced detail  
- Neutral and inclusive  

### 7.3 Content Rules
Composite Edition must:
- include essential trainer notes  
- include simplified learner explanations  
- include cultural context  
- avoid overload  
- avoid jargon unless necessary  

### 7.4 Formatting Rules
- Trainer notes must be collapsible or clearly separated  
- Cultural notes must be concise  
- Learner explanations must be primary  

### 7.5 Suppression Rules
Composite Edition must NOT:
- include full trainer detail  
- include full cultural depth  
- include overly technical language  

---

## 8. Edition Interaction Rules

### 8.1 Edition Isolation
Each edition must be independently valid.

### 8.2 Edition Derivation
All editions derive from the **same semantic core**.

### 8.3 Edition Conflict Resolution
If two edition rules conflict:
1. Semantic invariants win  
2. Structural invariants win  
3. Governance wins  
4. Edition‑specific rule applies last  

### 8.4 Edition Validation
Each edition must pass:
- OSF validation  
- DIS validation  
- governance validation  
- edition‑specific validation  

---

## 9. Edition Formatting Rules (Global)

### 9.1 Headings
- H1: Lesson title  
- H2: Sections  
- H3: Subsections  

### 9.2 Typography
- Bold: key actions  
- Italics: examples  
- Code blocks: never used in editions  

### 9.3 Lists
- Bullet lists for tasks  
- Numbered lists for sequences  

---

## 10. Summary
This file defines the **complete, authoritative rule‑set** for all Clarity Engine editions.  
These rules ensure that each edition:

- preserves meaning  
- preserves structure  
- adapts tone  
- adapts detail  
- adapts cultural context  
- remains fully governed  

Editions transform the same semantic core into **four distinct, audience‑aligned experiences**.
