# Youth Edition (v1) — Clarity Engine

## 1. Purpose
The Youth Edition adapts lesson content for younger learners (ages 10–17) while preserving:

- lesson structure  
- CEFR alignment  
- semantic meaning  
- domain isolation  
- edition isolation  

This is a Phase‑4 extension and does not modify system‑layer behaviour.

---

## 2. Edition Identity

### 2.1 Name
**Youth Edition**

### 2.2 Audience
- school‑age learners  
- teens in language programmes  
- young learners in private tutoring  
- early‑stage academic learners  

### 2.3 Purpose
To provide:
- simpler explanations  
- age‑appropriate examples  
- supportive tone  
- engaging tasks  
- safe, neutral content  

---

## 3. Edition Invariants

Youth Edition must:
- preserve lesson structure  
- preserve CEFR alignment  
- preserve core vocabulary targets  
- preserve functional language  
- remain edition‑isolated  

Youth Edition may **simplify**, but never **distort**, the semantic core.

---

## 4. Tone & Voice

### 4.1 Tone
- friendly  
- encouraging  
- clear  
- positive  

### 4.2 Voice
- supportive  
- age‑appropriate  
- non‑patronising  
- confidence‑building  

---

## 5. Content Expansions

Youth Edition must include:

### 5.1 Simplified Explanations
- shorter sentences  
- clearer examples  
- reduced abstraction  

### 5.2 Engagement Boosters
- relatable scenarios  
- simple challenges  
- short creative tasks  

### 5.3 Confidence Support
- positive reinforcement  
- success‑oriented phrasing  
- gentle correction framing  

### 5.4 Safety Filters
Content must avoid:
- violence  
- politics  
- sensitive social issues  
- adult themes  

---

## 6. Content Suppressions

Youth Edition must NOT include:
- corporate language  
- advanced academic structures  
- complex idioms  
- heavy cultural analysis  
- adult‑oriented examples  

---

## 7. Formatting Rules

### 7.1 Youth Blocks
Youth‑specific content must be clearly marked:

**YOUTH TIP:**  
**TRY THIS:**  
**FUN CHALLENGE:**  

### 7.2 Structure
- short paragraphs  
- simple bullet lists  
- clear examples  

### 7.3 Placement
Youth blocks appear:
- after explanations  
- after examples  
- at the end of sections  

---

## 8. Edition Interaction

### 8.1 With Trainer Edition
Trainer Edition may include youth‑specific guidance, but Youth Edition itself remains learner‑facing.

### 8.2 With Client Edition
Youth Edition is a specialised variant of Client Edition with:
- more scaffolding  
- more examples  
- more engagement  

### 8.3 With Cultural Edition
Youth Edition may include cultural notes **only if simplified and safe**.

### 8.4 With Composite Edition
Composite Edition may blend Youth Edition elements when appropriate.

---

## 9. Validation Rules

Youth Edition must pass:
- OSF validation  
- DIS validation  
- governance validation  
- edition isolation validation  
- CEFR alignment validation  

Forbidden:
- adult themes  
- sensitive topics  
- complex jargon  
- negative framing  

---

## 10. Summary
The Youth Edition provides a **simplified, supportive, age‑appropriate** version of each lesson.  
It is fully compliant with:

- system invariants  
- governance  
- meta rules  
- lesson rules  
- edition rules  

This is the fifth major extension in Phase‑4.
